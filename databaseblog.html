Database Blog Summary

In the article titled Data Observability: Is It Data Quality Monitoring or More?, the author Petr Travkin begins by describing what Observability is.

After that, Travkin constrasts the meaning of Observability with that of Monitoring, drawing a thin line of difference between the two similar, sometimes interchangeable terms.

The author asserts that Observability encompasses the collection of data metrics through the whole IT landscape, and Monitoring only looks for anticipated issues or abnormalies that may occur in the systems.

He then proceeds to discuss the extent of Observability, whether or not it should include data about infrastructure and applications in additions to pipelines data.
